Discussing Emotional Intelligence

EQ OR IQ?

Are Your Emotions Getting the Better of You?

December 06, 2018 by Natalie

In this post I will define what it means to be Emotionally Intelligent and highlight the importance of developing these skills for success in life

EQ or rather the Emotional Quotient is the capacity to not just be aware of our emotions and the emotions of others, but also how we control and express ourselves and the effect this has on other people.

Our emotions can drive our behaviour, impacting others (be it positive or negative) which is why it is important to understand how to manage them, not just for ourselves but others - especially when under pressure.

Emotional Intelligence is:

  • Indentifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling our own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Relating to others

The Intelligence Quotient differs to our EQ and while a high IQ is desirable, it does not guarantee success in life. A developed EQ will allow you to understand and control how others perceive you, and it is a skill which can be learned and strengthened.

The Intelligence Quotient is identified by:

  • Quantitative reasoning
  • Working & short-term memory
  • Visual & spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
