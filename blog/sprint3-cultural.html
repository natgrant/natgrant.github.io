Discussing Emotional Intelligence

EQ OR IQ?

Are Your Emotions Getting the Better of You?

December 06, 2018 by Natalie

In this post I will define what it is to be Emotionally Intelligent and highlight the importance of developing your EQ

EQ or rather the Emotional Quotient is the capacity to not just be aware of our emotions, but also how we control and express them.

Our emotions can drive our behaviour, impacting others, be it positive or negative, which is why it is important to understand how to manage emotions, not just for ourselves but others - especially when under pressure.

EQ is:

  • Indentifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling your own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Relating to others

The Intelligence Quotient differs to our EQ and while a high EQ is desirable, it does not guarantee success in life. EQ can in fact have a stronger impact in the path to success, and it can be learned and strengthened.

IQ is identified by:

  • Quantitative reasoning
  • Working & short-term memory
  • Visual & spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
